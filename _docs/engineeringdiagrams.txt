The main engineering diagrams used to analyze pumps (centrifugal and reciprocating) focus on hydraulic performance, system integration, mechanical design, and condition monitoring. These are standard in process plants, oil & gas, water treatment, and chemical facilities — and they tie directly into tools like Cognite (for contextualized data) and Fabric (for analytics/ML).Here’s the definitive list:1. Detailed information on K-value coefficients (for pumps)In pump engineering, K-value (also called resistance coefficient, minor loss coefficient, or K-factor) is the dimensionless number used to calculate localized pressure/head losses through fittings, valves, entrances, exits, bends, tees, reducers, etc.Formula (Darcy-Weisbach minor loss):
hf=K×v22gh_f = K \times \frac{v^2}{2g}h_f = K \times \frac{v^2}{2g}
hfh_fh_f
 = head loss (ft or m)
(v) = fluid velocity in the pipe (ft/s or m/s)
(g) = gravity (32.2 ft/s² or 9.81 m/s²)

These K-values are added to the friction losses (from pipe roughness) to build the system curve above.Key facts about K-value coefficientsStatic or very slowly changing → exactly the type of data you ingest once (or on change) into Cognite/PI AF as attributes on piping elements or in a Raw table/Data Model.
Values come from standardized tables (Crane TP-410, Idelchik Handbook, manufacturer data). They depend on:Fitting type and geometry (e.g., 90° elbow K ≈ 0.3–0.9)
Pipe size (sometimes Reynolds-number dependent)
Entrance/exit conditions

In your stack: Store K-values in PI AF attributes (linked to pipe segments) → Cognite PI AF extractor pulls them → Fabric Lakehouse uses them with real-time flow/pressure data to compute expected vs. actual head or detect fouling/leaks.

Note on possible confusion: This is not the thermodynamic VLE K-value (y/x) you may have used earlier for compressors/process simulation. In pumps (liquid service) it is almost always the hydraulic loss coefficient.These diagrams + K-coefficient data are the foundation for everything from basic sizing to predictive maintenance in Cognite + Fabric.
